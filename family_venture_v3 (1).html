The Family Assessment

Seven Questions. Seven Minutes.

Specific answers produce specific plans. The more honest you are about what's actually happening — not what you hope is happening — the sharper the output.

CLIENT NAME
Who is this plan for?
Your name or family name. Appears on the cover of your plan.
QUESTION 01
Describe your household: Who lives under your roof, what are 2–3 core family values, and what life stage are you in?
Be specific about people, values, and stage — this shapes every conclusion that follows.
QUESTION 02
What's the ONE biggest challenge or stressor in your family life or finances right now — and how long has it been unresolved?
Name the actual number or situation. Duration matters — chronic problems compound differently than acute ones.
QUESTION 03
In 3–5 years, where do you want your family to be — one specific financial milestone and one life or legacy change?
Not a range. A specific number. And a concrete legacy change — not a feeling or a vibe.
QUESTION 04
In the last 6–12 months: Where does most of your family time, energy, and money actually go — and where do you wish it went to build toward a venture?
The honest answer. Not the ideal. The gap between these two is where the plan lives.
QUESTION 05
On a scale of 1–10, how confident are you that your family could successfully launch and run a joint venture — and what one thing would raise that by 3 points?
Give the number first. Then explain what's driving it and what would actually change it.
QUESTION 06
If we delivered your tailored family venture plan today, what's the single first action your family would realistically take — and what might stop you?
Be honest about the stopper. "Time" is not a real answer — what's the actual blocker beneath it?
QUESTION 07 KEY QUESTION
What does your family know how to do better than most people — a skill, industry knowledge, or connection that others would genuinely pay for?
This is your unfair advantage. Every venture idea in your plan comes directly from this answer.
